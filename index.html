Instructions for Comparison Chart:

How to read the graph:
  • Old data is displayed in GREY; new data is displayed in BLUE
  • Legend for inputs:
    • inputFp - functional points (if functional points is over 1000, values will be displayed in thousands)
    • langSelect - language selected
    • inCpp - cost per person (hourly)
    • teamCongruity - team congruity (on a scale of 1 to 5, 1 being the lowest)
    • langExperience - language experience (on a scale of 1 to 5, 1 being the lowest)
    • platformExperience - platform experience (on a scale of 1 to 5, 1 being the lowest)
    • programerCap - programmer capability (on a scale of 1 to 5, 1 being the lowest)
    • testingSelect - testing and code review (on a scale of 1 to 5, 1 being the lowest)
    • effort - team effort (on a scale of 20% to 100%, 20% being the lowest)
    • complexity - project complexity (on a scale of 1 to 10, 1 being the lowest)

ABOUT

The following variables are used in the calculations to estimate the requirements of the project:

SLOC = Function Points * AvgSLOC(Language)
Effort (0.2-1) : Percentage of time dedicated to the project
Complexity (1 - 1.2) : Complexity of the project
Team Congruity (0.96 - 1.03) : How well does the team work together
Language Experience (0.84 - 1.20) : Experience in the specific language
Platform Experience (0.84 - 1.19) : Experience developing for the specific platform
Programmer Capability (0.76 - 1.33) : Programmer general experience
Testing and Review (0.85 - 1.20) : Amount spent testing and reviewing the code

Output formulas:
Person Month = constant(2.7) multiplied by the above variables
Cost = Person Month * Cost Per Month
Schedule = constant(3.5) * Person Month ^ Coefficient
Coefficient (0.3 - 0.34) is determined by logarithmic function based on SLOC
Maximum Defects are based on Function Points and Testing and Review Factor(0.85-1.20)
Documentation is an estimate equal to Function Points / 5
